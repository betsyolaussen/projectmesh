THE POTENTIAL PROBLEM

  • Mesh is a dialogue about how technology changes the way we communicate with each other and with ourselves.

  • When you are in a group of people who are more absorbed by their phones than by the people they are with.

  • When you mindlessly scroll through your phone, minutes or hours disappearing.

  • When you are able to build community with people across the world.

  • When you ghost or get ghosted.

  • When you pretend to be consumed by your screen to avoid a conversation with someone you recognize.

  • When you stay connected with friends and family who live in different time zones.

  • When you base your self worth off of the reactions you receive on social media.

  • When you text someone about your emotions rather than talking face to face.

  • When you have the ability to share your story, which helps someone else feel less alone.

WHY THINK ABOUT IT?

The use of technology and the role it plays in my relationships is something I find increasingly important to think about. Beginning during my undergraduate years in the Art program at the University of Wisconsin- Eau Claire, I have been making art about technology as a way to discuss and process how I feel technology impacts, both positively and negatively, my relationships and other human interactions I see in my communities.

Technology has advanced immensely in the last two hundred years, and personal-scale technology has advanced quite rapidly in the last thirty or so years. This has been personal computers, smartphones, watches, smart homes, classroom technology, and of course larger scale technological advances that impact medicine, travel, science, and military, which I focus on less in my work. As the presence of technology continues to build, the need to consider how technology interacts with and impacts our human society becomes more imperative.

In my work thus far, including Project MESH, I consider and discuss the presence and impact of technology on small scale interpersonal interactions-- the relationship with the self, with other individuals, or with small groups or communities. Technology can facilitate the building, deepening, or maintaining of interpersonal relationships, which is something we are all aware of at this point. However, it can also create distance, a feeling of absence, or even alienation in our relationships, both those we forge in person or online. I want to create a space where folks can discuss and share work about both the positive and negative outcomes of technology’s presence in our relationships, but I think it is particularly important to make space to think intentionally and discuss openly the way that technology sometimes hinders our ability to feel connected or present with one another.
